The man at the door is too familar, she thinks. He stands there alone in the cold and Ana greets him with a smirk, saying she is not so sure just the two of them will manage move to the piano by themselves. He begins to stutter excuses for the drivers of the truck stuck somewhere up north on a highway, but she smiles at him distractedly and turns to walk up the stairs. He pulls off his hat and follows her.

He feels moisture of sleet seep into his palms and mix with sweat as he twists the wool hat. His shoulders move as one shape, tensed in a tawny jacket, as he tries to angle his boots with each step up so as not to slip. He hears her ask something that sounds polite but not important, and begins to summon the air to ask her to repeat; but when he tries to speak he coughs, and she swivels to look into his face. She pauses to watch for a moment, her eyes alert and flashing with something between amusement and concern. He blushes. She slowly forms the words to ask if he wants tea.

He starts to slip and stutter through the noises of apology but she quickly interrupts in her own language, which she now knows is also his. She tells him not to worry; she knows they are always late. She feels a private pleasure at the trace of shock and recognition, confirming her guess before he speaks again. Her mouth stretches wide in a way that she can feel in her chest but she tries to hold it down, like a cork floating in a tub. He steps forward and she closes the door. They peer at each other in faint light.
